TAR 2017 Guide

Concepts/Notes

  • NEW (Optional Station): This year there will be 5 stations plus one optional station.
    • This optional station will not have a station leader and can be completed at any time during the race (but before cutoff time). The idea is to have the teams make the decision of whether to split off from the current path to gain more points.
    • A station sheet would be given to them with the questions they will need to answer. They will submit this sheet at the end of the race.
    • If teams are finished all 5 stations and there is still time before 1645 cutoff, suggest them to do the optional station.
  • NEW (Station/Transit Pass): Each team will get to choose one 'hint' or shortcut. They will need to decide which one to get at the beginning of the race.
    • The station pass, when used, would allow them to complete an easier version of the station. They will not know how it will be easier until the choose to use the pass at that station.
    • The transit pass, when used, would given them transit directions from one location to another.
    • These passes can only be used once, cannot be applied to the optional station and is worth zero points if kept until the end.
  • Format is points based. Each station has a total of 50 points (with some bonuses) and arriving at the finish line will also receive points. The team with the most points wins. Ties will be broken by tiebreaker question at the end.
  • All stations are designed to be roughly 30 minutes. There is no hard time limit, and they can choose not to complete the task (which gives them no points).
    • Technically, they can stay as long or as little as they want. If they are really stuck it would probably be best for them to move on. Simply arriving and leaving the station gives them 20 points.
    • With that said, teams can only submit once (with the exception of physical challenges at Stations B & D).
  • Lunch is done after Station C, at HTO Park by Harbourfront. On this day, there is a Waterfront Artisan Market where they can buy food. If not, they can walk about a block in either direction for Tims, Subway and Pizza Pizza.
  • Cutoff is at 1645. Instructions to get to the end is with the Team Leaders.
  • At the end, their time is stopped once they've handed in the puzzles and the feedback page.
    • Additional points are awarded for teams that finish. The earlier you finish, the more points earned.
    • They may still continue with the puzzles, but they will still be timed.

Locations/Directions

Directions are based of the teams running the race.

Start: STC

  • In front of the theatres

Station A: Toronto Reference Library

  • Subway to Bloor-Yonge, then head north to the library.
  • Station leaders should sit by the tables by the edge so they can be seen from the center.

Station B: Ryerson's Kerr Hall Field

  • Subway to Dundas, then head to northeast to the field inside Kerr Hall
  • Station leaders should have a seat on the hill or on the benches around

Station C: HTO Park by Harbourfront

  • Subway to Union, then take 509/510 streetcar and stop at Rees Street. Cross the street then walk a little bit west.
  • Station leaders should either sit by the hills or by the sand.

Station D: Cherry Beach

  • Two options, both of which requires taking 121C eastbound.
    • Take 510 heading up and stopping at Bremner Blvd, then take 121C
    • Or streetcar back to Union station, exit the station and wait for the 121C at Bay and Front (Southeast corner)
  • Station leaders should sit by a place with pile-able sand (ie. dry sand that is playable). Hopefully there is space by the far end of the parking lot (Closer one is currently swamped last time I checked).

Station E: Distillery District

  • If coming up from Cherry Beach: Take 121C back up and stop at Mill street. It would say 'Detour on Route' because of construction on the actual Mill Street, so it will actually drop you off at Parliament Street
  • Otherwise, you can take a 514 streetcar eastbound which would drop you off at the Distillery streetcar loop.
  • Station leaders should sit by the large flask/beaker sculpture.

End: Milliken Park Community Recreation Center

  • Simpliest way that doesn't involve switching is to take the 514 streetcar to King Station, then head to Finch and take 53 eastbound.
    • Can also decide to go to STC and take 129, but there is subway closure on Line 3, so hopefully they know this.
    • Can also walk up to King to be able to catch 504 streetcar in addition to 514, but I'm not sure how much additional time would be saved.

Web App

  • Used last year and still most likely buggy; try not to break it please (you can try after the race).
  • Usernames and passwords are given out with the respective packages.
  • Using the browser's refresh page will most likely log you out; use the provided refresh button or swap between pages.
  • Front page: overall progress of the teams. Has timestamps for when teams arrived and left the stations.
  • Station Leaders: each station has its own page; will be visible with appropriate username/password.
    • The station's instructions as well as the scoring will be on this page.
    • When a team arrives, click on the appropriate team and click 'Arrived'.
    • When a team leaves, click on 'Left'.
    • You can only enter a score after the team has left. You can change the score if you entered it incorrectly.
  • Team Leaders: each team has its own page. This page only contains the progress of your team. Is only visible with appropriate username/password.
    • When your team has reached the end location, you must then use the page and click 'Finish Race'. [NOTE: You can only do so once they have handed over the feedback page and the puzzles. They can still continue with the puzzles, but they are still being timed.]
  • In the event that the web app is down:
    • Use this page as backup for information.
    • Station Leaders: mark down the scores (either on this page or on their sheets)
    • Team Leaders: mark down their finish times on the feedback page.

Station B

Location: Ryerson's Kerr Hall Field. Subway to Dundas, then head northeast to Victoria St, then turn right on Gould St. Enter the Kerr Hall field on your left. Ideally sit in a spot with a grassy field.

Expected Arrival Time: 1115

Instructions:

  • When they arrive, mark their time (using the app) or on a separate sheet. There is no instructions to give them; you will explain the task.
  • Teams will have to go through 3 stages of holding various yoga poses. Once they complete the stage with the required time, only then can they may move to the next stage.
    • 3 sheets of pictures will also be in your package; you can use them to show to the teams.
  • If you find teams struglling with the task for a while, you may simplify the stage, but reduce the amount of available points they can earn. How you simplify or reduce the points is up to your judgement. They may also choose to give up if it has been a while (Recommended time for each station is 30 minutes).
  • Once they complete all three stages, or they decide to give up, you may mark their time and give them their next clue.
  • Station Pass: Teams may submit their Station Pass to you at any point during the station. Only when they do, you can tell them the following (depending on their stage):
    • Stage 1: Only 2 people need to do one of the poses.
    • Stage 2: Skipped. Points will be redistributed.
    • Stage 3: Time reduced to 4 seconds.

Scoring: Points are in square brackets.

Regular

  1. [10] Arrive at Station B
  2. [3 each x 2 = 6 max] Stage 1 complete (2 poses)
  3. [10] Stage 2 complete
  4. [14] Stage 3 complete
  5. [10] Left Station B

Station Pass

  1. [10] Arrive at Station B
  2. [11] Stage 1 complete (1 pose)
  3. [19] Stage 3 complete
  4. [10] Left Station B

Total: 50 points

Station D

Location: Cherry Beach. Take the 121C eastbound all the way to the beach. On arrival, pick a spot with dry, fillable sand.

Expected Arrival Time: 1340

Instructions:

  • When they arrive, mark their time (using the app) or on a separate sheet. There is no instructions to give them; you will explain the task.
  • Teams will be physically playing the bucket puzzle. You will be giving them three different cups, 7 ounces, 9 ounces and 11 ounces. Their goal is to get 1 fluid ounce of sand. They may only do the following actions:
    • Completely fill up one cup with sand (all the way to the top).
    • Completely empty one cup (back on the beach).
    • Fill one cup to another cup. Either fill the second cup all the way to the top (and leave the rest in the first cup), or fill it up as much as possible.
      • They cannot spill or paritally pour.
  • Let them know that scoring is based on the least number of actions. They may try as many times as they like, and the last successful attempt will be counted.
  • Cutoff time is 1645. If they don't submit before this time, the points will not be counted. (Will get arrival and leaving points).
  • You can remind them the following:
    • There is no time limit. Recommended time is 30 mins.
    • If they haven't finished all the tasks, they may choose to leave early if they wish.
    • Cutoff time is 1645. If they don't submit before this time, the points will not be counted. (Will get arrival and leaving points).
  • You will be given a cup with the markings to validate the attempt. While it does not need to be exactly on the line, it should not be to little or too much.
  • Station Pass: Teams may submit their Station Pass to you at any point during the station. Only when they do, you can tell them the following:
    • They will need 5 ounces instead of one ounce.

Scoring: Points are denoted in square brackets.

Regular

  1. [10] Arrive at Station D
  2. [Various] 7 moves = 30 points, 8-10 moves = 25 points, 11-15 moves = 20 points, 16+ moves = 15 points
  3. [10] Left Station D

Station Pass

  2. [Various] 4 moves = 30 points, 5-8 moves = 25 points, 9-12 moves = 20 points, 13+ moves = 15 points
  3. [10] Left Station D

Instructions:

  • When they arrive, mark their time (using the app), find their sheet, mark the same time, and pass the sheet to them.
  • For this station, teams will need to navigate around Distillery District finding historic artifacts.
    • There is a total of 9 artifacts, each one located in one of the 9 heritage exhibits (the 4 yellow diamonds icon).
    • They will just need to find and submit the name of the artifact.
    • They will only need 8 correct answers to receive full marks. The ninth correct answer will earn them bonus marks.
  • Cutoff time is 1645. If they don't submit before this time, the points will not be counted. (Will get arrival and leaving points).
  • You can remind them the following:
    • There is no time limit. Recommended time is 30 mins.
    • If they haven't finished all the tasks, they may choose to leave early if they wish.
    • Cutoff time is 1645. If they don't submit before this time, the points will not be counted. (Will get arrival and leaving points).
  • Station Pass: Teams may submit their Station Pass to you at any point during the station. Only when they do, you can tell them the following:
    • They will need to correctly identify 6 correct artifacts to receive full marks.
    • The next 3 correct answers will be bonus.

End

Location: Milliken Park Community Recreation Centre. 4325 McCowan Rd. To head to the subway station either take 514 to King, 504 to King (head west) or Broadview (head east), or 121C to King (if at Station D). From there:

  • Option 1: Subway to Finch and take 53 eastbound to McCowan Road, then walk south.
  • Option 2: Subway to Scarborough Centre and take 129 northbound to 4325 McCowan Road and walk.

Expected Arrival Time: 1830

Instructions:

  • When they finish the last station (or at cutoff), team leaders will hand them their last clue.
  • Their race officially ends when the following conditions hold:
    • Team arrives in front of community center.
    • Team hands over the feedback page to the Scouter.
    • Team hands over the puzzles page to the Scouter.
    • Team leader marks down the time on the app/writes the time on the feedback sheet.
  • If they arrive at the end, they can still work on the puzzles, but the time will still continue.
  • Points are awarded for finishing the race. The earlier they finish, they more points awarded.

Scoring:

  • [25] Awarded for team that arrives first
  • [20] Awarded for team that arrives second
  • [15] Awarded for team that arrives third
  • [13] Awarded for team that arrives fourth
  • [12] Awarded for team that arrives fifth/sixth
