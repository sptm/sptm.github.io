Concepts/Notes

  • All locations have been given to them at the start of the race, but its encoded.
    • Teams will be given instructions to decode. Once they have decrypted the location, they must then go there, complete the station to be given instructions to decoding the next location.
    • Station Leaders: when they arrive at your station, you may choose to have them show their work on decoding. You can choose to not let them proceed until work is shown (or they understand what is going on).
    • All encoding is done by shifting the letters a certain amount. As an example, ROT13 means to rotate/shift by 13 letters (A to N, B to O, etc.). Similarly, ROT10 means to rotate by 10 letters. A decoding circle will be given to teams to help with decoding.
  • All locations are new. Each one is near a subway station and from there either be a walk or one additional streetcar.
  • Format is points based. Each station has a total of 50 points (with some bonuses) and arriving at the finish line will also receive points. The team with the most points wins. Ties will be broken by tiebreaker question at the end.
    • The points breakdown is a secret until the end of the race.
  • All stations are designed to be roughly 30 minutes. There is no hard time limit, and they can choose not to complete the task (which gives them no points).
    • Technically, they can stay as long or as little as they want. If they are really stuck it would probably be best for them to move on. Simply arriving and leaving the station gives them 20 points.
    • With that said, teams can only submit once (with the exception of physical challenges at Stations B & E).
  • Lunch is done along the way. Recomended time is to have lunch before entering High Park (Station D), as there may not be food services available at High Park.
  • Cutoff is at 1700. Instructions to get to the end is with the Team Leaders.
  • At the end, their time is stopped once they’ve handed in the puzzles and the feedback page.
    • Additional points are awarded for teams that finish. The earlier you finish, the more points earned.
    • They may still continue with the puzzles, but they will still be timed.

Web App

  • Experiemental and most likely buggy; try not to break it please (you can try after the race).
  • Usernames and passwords are given out with the respective packages.
  • Using the browser’s refresh page will most likely log you out; use the provided refresh button or swap between pages.
  • Front page: overall progress of the teams. Has timestamps for when teams arrived and left the stations.
  • Station Leaders: each station has its own page; will be visible with appropriate username/password.
    • The station’s instructions as well as the scoring will be on this page.
    • When a team arrives, click on the appropriate team and click ‘Arrived’.
    • When a team leaves, click on ‘Left’.
    • You can only enter a score after the team has left. You can change the score if you entered it incorrectly.
  • Team Leaders: each team has its own page. This page only contains the progress of your team. Is only visible with appropriate username/password.
    • When your team has reached the end location, you must then use the page and click ‘Finish Race’. [NOTE: You can only do so once they have handed over the feedback page and the puzzles. They can still continue with the puzzles, but they are still being timed.]
  • In the event that the web app is down:
    • Use this page as backup for information.
    • Station Leaders: mark down the scores (either on this page or on their sheets)
    • Team Leaders: mark down their finish times on the feedback page.

Stations

Start

Location: STC; in front of theatre

Expected Arrival Time: 0900

Instructions:

  • Teams will be given an instuructions/rules sheet in the form of fill in the blanks/true or false/multiple choice.
  • Once they’ve completed the questions, they must then find the team leader with the correct arrangement of 2x2 hands (each team leader is holding a 12x12 grid).
  • Once they’ve found the correct leader (the leader has to verify), the leader then checks their answers. If correct, the team leader passes on the decoding sheet. If answers are wrong, they must try again.

Scoring: None.

Expected Arrival Time: 0920

Instructions:

    • You can only submit once.
  • Once they have submitted, mark their time on the sheet, record the time on the app, and tell the team ROT7.

Station B

Location: Billy Bishop Airport. Subway to Union Station, then 509 Harboufront to Bathurst Street. Then walk to airport and go through the tunnel. Go up the escalator and there should be a seating area on the right.

Expected Arrival Time: 1135

Instructions:

  • When teams arrive, you may ask them to show their decoding work. The phrase is BILLY BISHOP AIRPORT. If you are satisfied, they may continue.
  • After marking their time (using the app), find their sheet, mark the same time, and pass the sheet to them.
  • The general idea is to move everyone from one side to the other. The four passengers each have restrictions
  • They can only move either zero or one person at a time. After movement, the following restrictions must be met:
    • Clumsy archaeologist cannot be with Mummy by his/herself; If the senior archaeologist is there then it is fine.
    • Thief cannot be with any of the archaeologists by his/herself; If the pilot is on the same side then it is fine.
    • As long as these restrictions are met, any other scenarios is fine.
  • The goal is to do this in the least number of moves possible (answer below). Your job is to track the number of moves (Moving the plane back with no passengers is valid and still counts as a move). They may try as many times as they wish, but the most recent attempt will be recorded.
  • Once they are satisfied, mark their time and number of moves on the sheet, record the time on the app, and tell the team ROT1.
  • You can remind them the following:
    • There is no time limit. Recommended time is 30 mins.
    • You can attempt as many times as you wish, but the most recent attempt will be recorded.

Scoring: Points are in square brackets.

  1. [10] Arrive at Station B
  2. [57-3*x] Where x is the total number of moves. The optimal number of moves is 9, So the highest score they can get is 30. Subtracts 3 points for every extra move made, down to 0.
  3. [10] Left Station B

Total: 50 points

Solution: The best solution is 9 moves.

Station E

Location: High Park Hillside Gardens. Subway to High Park Station and either walk or take the seasonal bus to the High Park Loop. At the end of the loop, head west down the hill to the maple leaf. Find one of the benches around here.

Expected Arrival Time: 1435

Instructions:

  • When teams arrive, you may ask them to show their decoding work. The phrase is HIGH PARK HILLSIDE GARDENS. If you are satisfied, you may mark their time (using the app) or on a separate sheet.
  • There is no instructions sheet to give to the teams. You will need to explain the tasks.
  • This station is divided into 3 tasks (details below). Once they have completed the task, you may explain the next task. Once they have completed all tasks, mark their time (using the app) and tell the team ROT19.
  • Cutoff time is 1700. If they don’t submit before this time, the points will not be counted. (Will get arrival and leaving points).
  • You can remind them the following:
    • There is no time limit. Recommended time is 30 mins.
    • If they haven’t finished all the tasks, they may choose to leave early if they wish.
    • Cutoff time is 1700. If they don’t submit before this time, the points will not be counted. (Will get arrival and leaving points).

Tasks:

  1. Have the team members form a circle facing inwards with their arms out. You must then mix/rearrange their hands so that they are holding someone else’s. Their goal is to move around and untangle, without letting go, so that they form a circle.
  2. Same as above, but this time one person from the team is outside the circle and is designated as the caller. The rest of the members follow the same instructions as above, but they are now blindfolded. Should multiple teams be waiting and there are not enough blindfolds, impose a 3 minute time limit for the team before they switch out (otherwise there is no time limit).
  3. Save as above, but this time all team members are in the circle and blindfolded. Should multiple teams be waiting and there are not enough blindfolds, impose a 5 minute time limit for the team before they switch out (otherwise there is no time limit).

Scoring: Points are denoted in square brackets.

  1. [10] Arrive at Station E
  2. [6] Task 1 complete
  3. [10] Task 2 complete
  4. [14] Task 3 complete
  5. [10] Left Station E

Total: 50 points
